Cottage Pie

Active Time:

Total Time:

Serves:

← Back to

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 500g beef (you can use any of the cuts of beef for this. Leftover roast is great, ground beef works well, but we like to use the instant pot to get all the meat off the fiddly little maui ribs.)
  • 1 Tbsp chutney or relish
  • 2 Tbsp flour
  • 1 Tbsp tomato purée
  • ¾ cup beef stock
  • 1 Tbsp chutney or relish
  • Assorted vegetables (optional)
  • Puff pastry

Directions

  1. Put the oil and onion in a large saucepan and cook very gently, stirring occasionally, until the onion is translucent and tender – about 10 minutes.
  2. Add the meat. (If you're using raw meat, add it and cook until just browned, stirring constantly.)
  3. Add the flour and cook for 1 minute, stirring, then add the tomato purée, stock and chutney or relish.
  4. Preheat the oven to 190ºC (375ºF).
  5. Bring to a simmer and cook gently for 15–20 minutes.
  6. Roll out your pastry (cottage pie is traditionally topped with mashed potato but I'm a sucker for pastry).
  7. Lie your pie dish with half the pastry (you can skip this if you just want the upper crust).
  8. Spoon the meat mixture into a pie dish. If you want to add any soft vegetables (peas, red pepper, etc) gently stir them in now.
  9. Place the top pastry on the pie and cut some slits for the steam.
  10. Bake for 20 minutes or until the top is crisp and golden.
